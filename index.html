Dharmic Traditions in Stone

Over 1,500 years of epigraphic evidence reveals the living continuity of India's philosophical, religious and cultural traditions —
from early Vedic yajnas to the great Agamic temple civilisations of South India.

Sacred Traditions
🕉️
Advaita Vedānta
Non-Dual Philosophy
Inscriptions document Shankaracharya's mathas, Vedantic debate traditions, and the spread of Advaita philosophy from Kerala to Kashmir.
🔱
Shaiva Āgama
Tradition of Shiva
From Pashupata seals to Lingayat copper plates — temple grants, pilgrim records, and Shaiva theological inscriptions across the Deccan and Tamil Nadu.
🪷
Vaishnava Traditions
Bhakti & Pāñcharātra
Alvar saints, Ramanuja's Vishishtadvaita, Madhva's Dvaita — Vaishnava temple charters from Chola, Pallava and Vijayanagara periods.
🔥
Vedic Yajña Tradition
Ritual & Sacrifice
Agnihotra, Ashvamedha, Vajapeya records — epigraphic evidence of great Vedic sacrificial traditions performed by kings and Brahmin communities.
☸️
Jaina Tradition
Ahimsa & Liberation
Epigraphia Carnatica's richest corpus — Gomateshvara, Ganga dynasty records, Digambara matha traditions across Karnataka.
🙏
Buddhist Heritage
Dhamma & Sangha
Ashokan edicts, stupa donative records at Sanchi, Amaravati, Nagarjunakonda — the epigraphic foundations of the Buddhist world.
Epigraphic Timeline of India
c. 300 BCE
Ashokan Brahmi Edicts
Emperor Ashoka's rock and pillar edicts mark the dawn of systematic Indian epigraphy. Dhamma proclamations inscribed in Brahmi across the subcontinent.
1st–3rd Century CE
Satavahana & Kushana Records
Buddhist donative inscriptions at Sanchi, Amaravati, Nagarjunakonda. Trade-routes, guilds, and royal gifts documented in Brahmi and Kharosthi scripts.
4th–6th Century CE
Gupta Golden Age
Elaborate Sanskrit prashastis, land grants, and temple endowments. Vedic yajnas revived under Samudragupta. Shaiva-Vaishnava temple-building begins.
6th–8th Century CE
Chalukya & Pallava Florescence
Badami cave inscriptions, Mahendravarman's rock-cut temples, Mahabalipuram. Kannada, Telugu and Tamil scripts crystallise from Brahmi.
8th–10th Century CE
Rashtrakuta Empire & Advaita Tradition
Rashtrakuta copper-plate grants document wide religious pluralism across the Deccan. Inscriptions in this period reference Advaita Vedanta mathas and Shaiva-Vaishnava endowments — dates for individual figures shown here are drawn from EI records only. [EI corpus, 8th–10th c. records]
9th–13th Century CE
Chola Temple Civilisation
Thousands of inscriptions at Brihadeeshvara, Gangaikondacholapuram — devadana land grants, devadasi services, and elaborate Agamic rites.
12th–14th Century CE
Hoysala, Kakatiya & Reform Movements
Kannada and Telugu inscriptions from Belur, Halebidu, Warangal. Basavanna's Veerashaiva reform and Ramanujacharya's Vishishtadvaita documented in stone.
14th–17th Century CE
Vijayanagara — Last Great Hindu Empire
Hampi inscriptions document the empire's vast temple economy, Sanskrit and Kannada court culture, Madhva Vedanta patronage, and classical performing arts.
